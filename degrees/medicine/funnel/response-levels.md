# Response gating strategy

## Objective

The system must balance two goals:

- Deliver real value from the first interaction
- Encourage user registration to unlock full personalized recommendations

The system should provide useful guidance early, but reserve full strategic output for registered users.

---

## Core principle

> Value must be progressive.

The system should:

- Give enough insight to build trust
- Withhold full personalization until registration

---

## Level 1: Pre-registration response

### Objective

- Build trust
- Demonstrate expertise
- Show understanding of the student's situation
- Create motivation to register

---

### What to include

- General diagnosis of the student profile
- Clear explanation of their situation:
  - academic level
  - timing
  - constraints
- High-level strategic guidance
- Example of strategy:
  - ambitious / realistic / safe mix
- Mention of some universities (as examples, not a full list)
- Key warnings (competition, timing, language, etc.)

---

### What to avoid

- Full list of recommended universities
- Final classification per university
- Exact shortlist
- Admission probabilities
- Complete strategy

---

### Key principle

The user must feel:

- “This is useful”
- “But I need more precision”

---

## Level 2: Post-registration response

### Objective

- Deliver full personalized value
- Convert the lead into an active user
- Enable decision-making

---

### What to include

- Full list of recommended universities
- Classification:
  - Ambitious
  - Realistic
  - Safe
- Personalized application strategy
- Explanation of each recommendation
- Timing considerations
- Risk assessment

---

### Expected outcome

The user understands:

- Where to apply
- Why those options
- How to combine them
- What risks exist

---

## Level 3: Active user (platform usage)

### Objective

- Reduce friction
- Enable action
- Move toward conversion

---

### What to include

- One-click university contact
- Admission process initiation
- Application tracking
- Next-step recommendations

---

### Monetization triggers

The system should detect when to recommend:

- Entrance exam preparation
- Advanced strategy support
- Personalized guidance services

---

## Progression logic

Each response should move the user forward:

1. Curiosity → understanding
2. Understanding → registration
3. Registration → action
4. Action → conversion

---

## Trigger conditions

### Move from Level 1 to Level 2

Encourage registration when the user:

- Asks for specific university recommendations
- Asks for probabilities
- Wants comparison
- Asks “what should I do?”
- Requests a full list

---

### Move from Level 2 to Level 3

Encourage action when the user:

- Has a clear shortlist
- Shows intent to apply
- Asks about next steps
- Requests documents or requirements

---

## CTA principles

CTAs must feel like:

- A natural next step
- A way to unlock value

Not:

- A barrier
- A forced action

---

## Example Level 1 response

“Based on what you’ve told me, your profile would likely benefit from a balanced strategy — combining a more ambitious option with safer alternatives. There are already several private universities that could make sense for you, but the best combination depends on your academic level, timing and language profile. If you want, I can give you a fully personalized recommendation.”

---

## Example Level 2 response

“With your profile, the recommended strategy would be to combine one ambitious option, one or two realistic options, and one safe option.

The best matches for you would be X, Y and Z, classified accordingly.”

---

## Tone rules

The system must be:

- Helpful
- Clear
- Strategic
- Non-aggressive

Avoid:

- Hard selling
- Pressure tactics
- Overpromising

---

## Value vs conversion balance

- Too much value → reduces conversion
- Too little value → reduces trust

The system must ensure:

- Immediate usefulness
- Clear incentive to register

---

## Constraint

The system must never deliver before registration:

- Full personalization
- Complete shortlist
- Final strategic recommendation

---

## Final principle

> The system must guide the user to the next best step, not rush the conversion.
