# Universidad Nebrija — Grado en Medicina

## Overview

Universidad Nebrija's Grado en Medicina, launched in the 2024-2025 academic year at the Campus de Ciencias de la Vida en La Berzosa (Comunidad de Madrid), represents the most structurally distinctive admission model among private medicine programs in Spain: rather than a written knowledge exam testing Biology, Chemistry and Mathematics, admission is based on a psychometric test, an English level assessment and the academic record. This makes Nebrija strategically accessible to candidates with strong intellectual and vocational profiles who underperform specifically on written science examinations.

The degree is fully taught in Spanish, follows a competency-based and system-integrated curriculum across 6 years and 360 ECTS, incorporates 58 ECTS of supervised clinical rotations beginning in Year 2 Semester 2, and provides access to a clinical simulation center on campus. As of intake 2026-2027, the program is entering its third cohort. No MIR data or graduate outcomes are yet available.

The campus is located approximately 35–40 km northwest of Madrid city center. This rural setting is an important practical variable for students and families to factor into logistics and accommodation planning.

## Program Options

Nebrija offers a single Medicine program at the Campus de Ciencias de la Vida en La Berzosa. There is no online, hybrid or bilingual track. All 360 ECTS are taught entirely in Spanish on the La Berzosa campus.

Students enrolled in the Grado en Medicina have access to the **Diploma in English Professional Communication**, an additional credential offered by Nebrija alongside the degree. This diploma provides structured English language and professional communication training as a supplementary qualification within an otherwise fully Spanish-language program.

## Program Structure

The curriculum is organized across 6 academic years totaling 360 ECTS, with clinical exposure beginning in Year 2 Semester 2:

- **Year 1** (60 ECTS): Transversal and cross-system foundations — Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, Cell Biology, Genetics and Immunology, Pharmacology, Epidemiology and Preventive Medicine, Medical History and Medical Anthropology, Bioethics, Forensic Medicine, and general Pathophysiology.
- **Year 2** (clinical rotations begin in Semester 2): Preventive Medicine, Orthopaedics, Rheumatology, Cardiology, Pneumology, Cardiovascular and Thoracic Surgery, Internal Medicine (ER / ICU / OR).
- **Year 3**: General and Digestive Surgery, Urology, Nephrology, Internal Medicine rotations.
- **Year 4**: Neurosurgery, Neurohabilitation, Neurology, Ophthalmology, ENT, Psychiatry, Endocrinology, Nutrition, Internal Medicine.
- **Year 5**: OB/GYN, Geriatrics, Palliative Care, Paediatrics, Oncology, Haematology, Pathological Anatomy, Allergology, Dermatology, Emergency (obstetric, gynaecological, paediatric).
- **Year 6**: Primary Care, Emergency Services, ICU, Maxillofacial / Plastic and Reparative Surgery, Medical Research Foundation, Radiophysics, Nuclear Medicine, Radiation Oncology, Occupational Health Prevention.

Key structural figures:
- **Prácticas Clínicas Tuteladas (PCT):** 58 ECTS (from Year 2 Semester 2 through Year 6 Semester 1)
- **Trabajo Fin de Grado (TFG):** 6 ECTS (Year 6)
- **Curriculum modules:** 6

## Teaching Methodology

Nebrija positions its pedagogical model around three pillars: early and continuous clinical contact, small-group personalised learning, and simulation-based skills development.

Clinical integration begins in Year 2 (earlier than many traditional structures), with students accessing hospital environments alongside simulation labs from the second semester. The university operates a **Centro de Simulación Clínica Avanzada** on the La Berzosa campus — a hospital-grade facility designed to train technical and humanistic clinical skills in a controlled and safe environment.

The curriculum is built as competency-based and system-integrated rather than discipline-by-discipline, with cross-subject coordinators ensuring pedagogical coherence between theoretical and practical learning. This design, the university notes, mirrors models applied in internationally recognized medicine schools.

As **Socio Benefactor of the Real Academia Nacional de Medicina de España (RANME)**, Nebrija grants all Medicine professors and students access to RANME scientific sessions, conferences and terminology/museographic initiatives — an academic credentialing layer not common among recently founded medicine programs.

## Admission Model

Nebrija's Medicine admission model is the most structurally distinct among Spanish private medicine schools: there is no written academic knowledge exam. Candidates are not tested on Biology, Chemistry, Physics or Mathematics.

Admission is based on three components:

**1. Academic record** — Year 1 Bachillerato final grades and in-progress Year 2 Bachillerato grades submitted at the candidacy stage. Required track: Ciencias de la Salud or Ciencias. Evaluated by the Comisión de Admisiones. No explicit minimum grade for admission is published (the scholarship threshold of ≥9.0 nota media + ≥12.6 EVAU applies only to Becas de Excelencia applicants, not to admission itself).

**2. Prueba psicométrica** — An onsite psychometric assessment evaluating aptitude and personal/vocational profile, administered at Campus de Ciencias de la Vida en La Berzosa or Campus Princesa (as assigned). The exact format, content and scoring methodology are not publicly disclosed.

**3. Prueba de nivel de inglés** — An onsite English proficiency test, also at the assigned campus, or replaced by an ACLES-recognized official English certificate submitted with the original application. Candidates who submit a valid ACLES certificate are exempt from sitting the onsite test.

Published weightings across all three components: PENDING_CONFIRMATION. The relative contribution of each component to the final admission decision is not disclosed.

The strategic implication is significant: candidates who have invested heavily in science exam preparation for other schools (UNAV, UIC, UAX) hold no scoring advantage at Nebrija. Equally, candidates with strong psychometric profiles and existing English credentials can compete effectively regardless of their science exam history.

## Admission Process

The admission process follows four sequential steps:

**Step 1 — Submit candidacy (Presenta tu candidatura)**
Send all required documents in a single email to: **admisionmedicina@nebrija.es**
Subject line: full name of the candidate. Required documents:
- Solicitud de Admisión (form downloadable from the Nebrija website)
- Copy of DNI or Passport
- Academic record: Y1 Bachillerato final grades + Y2 Bachillerato in-progress grades at time of application
- Carta de Motivación (downloadable template)
- Official ACLES-recognized English certificate, if held

After document review and verification of legal access requirements, the university sends a written confirmation of the test appointment date.

**Step 2 — Take admission tests (Realiza las pruebas de admisión)**
Both tests are presential at Campus de Ciencias de la Vida en La Berzosa or Campus Princesa in Madrid (assigned by the university in the confirmation letter):
1. Prueba psicométrica
2. Prueba de nivel de inglés (waived for candidates with a valid ACLES certificate submitted in Step 1)

Physical photo ID is required on the day. Candidates without a written confirmation from the university may not sit the tests.

**Step 3 — Receive results (Resultado de las pruebas)**
Results are communicated in writing within 10 days of the test date.

**Step 4 — Formalise place (Formaliza tu reserva de plaza)**
Admitted candidates have 10 days to pay the 3,000 EUR reservation deposit. The deposit is deductible from Year 1 annual tuition. It is non-refundable except if the candidate fails bachillerato/EvAU in the June call, or in cases of documented serious medical conditions preventing enrolment.

## Admission Score Distribution

Because no admission weightings are published, a precise score distribution analysis is not possible for this program. The following observations apply:

- **No academic exam**: Biology, Chemistry and Mathematics preparation does not generate any scoring advantage. Students whose primary competitive strength is science exam performance are on equal footing at Nebrija with students who have not specialized in those subjects.
- **Psychometric test centrality**: With no knowledge exam, the prueba psicométrica is likely the most differentiating element. Its exact format is not disclosed, but psychometric assessments in Spanish university admissions typically evaluate logical reasoning, aptitude, and vocational/motivational alignment.
- **English test or certificate**: Holding a valid ACLES certificate exempts from the onsite English test. Whether this generates a scoring advantage versus merely fulfilling a requirement is PENDING_CONFIRMATION.

- **No historical cut-off data**: The degree is in its third cohort. No benchmark scores exist to estimate what level of combined performance secures admission.

## Accommodation and Cost of Living

The Campus de Ciencias de la Vida is located in La Berzosa, a small municipality near Hoyo de Manzanares, approximately 35–40 km northwest of Madrid city center. This is a rural campus setting, not embedded in an urban environment.

**Practical considerations:**

- Students based in central Madrid need to commute daily. A realistic round-trip travel time is 1–1.5 hours depending on transport mode and origin point.
- The La Berzosa / Hoyo de Manzanares area is significantly more affordable for accommodation than central Madrid, but student housing infrastructure in the area is limited.
- Access by car is practical; public transport connections to the campus are limited compared to Madrid's urban campuses.
- Students who prefer urban living and do not drive should factor in commuting costs and time as a real daily constraint over 6 years.

## Strategic Fit

### Good Fit For

- Students with strong psychometric aptitude and vocational commitment who do not perform optimally on written science knowledge exams (Biology, Chemistry, Mathematics)
- Students who already hold an ACLES-recognized English certificate (Cambridge, IELTS, etc.) and can use it to exempt the English test and possibly gain an admission advantage
- Students seeking a safety option with meaningfully lower current competition than established schools (UNAV, UIC, UAX)
- Candidates with strong academic records who want to minimize the variance associated with science-based written exams on test day
- Students based in the northwest Comunidad de Madrid for whom La Berzosa is logistically accessible
- Students who are also applying to other Spanish private medicine programs and want a structurally differentiated backup with no exam overlap preparation required

### Less Suitable For

- Students whose primary competitive strength is a very strong Biology/Chemistry exam score — this advantage is neutralized entirely
- Students with confirmed career intentions in WFME-requiring countries (USA, Canada, Australia) — WFME status is unconfirmed
- Students who require an urban campus environment or cannot feasibly commute to La Berzosa
- Students with a strong preference for a bilingual or English-medium program
- Students who prioritize historical MIR data as a proxy for program quality before committing

## Risks and Considerations

1. **WFME accreditation unconfirmed**: This is the most material strategic risk. WFME status is PENDING_CONFIRMATION. For students with international career aspirations — particularly in the US, Canada, Australia — this must be clarified directly with the university before enrolment.

2. **No track record**: The program is in its third cohort. No graduate outcomes, MIR performance data, dropout rates or clinical training quality data exist. Commitment requires trusting the institution's trajectory and infrastructure rather than evidenced results.

3. **Psychometric test opacity**: The exact format, scoring and relative weight of the prueba psicométrica are not published. There is no official sample material or practice format. Preparation is structurally harder to standardise than preparation for a syllabus-based science exam.

4. **Rural campus logistics**: La Berzosa is a rural campus with limited public transport from Madrid. Students who do not drive may face material daily logistical challenges across a 6-year degree.

5. **No published cut-off data**: Without historical admission statistics, it is not possible to predict the minimum performance needed for admission. Demand-driven competitiveness increases cannot be anticipated.

6. **Single-payment discount exclusion**: Nebrija's 2% single-payment tuition discount explicitly does not apply to Health Sciences programs. Students and families should not incorporate this into financial planning.

7. **Exchange availability**: International exchange places are ANNUAL_UPDATE_REQUIRED. Confirmed seats per year with FIU, Ponce and UIA are not published.

## Key Insight

Nebrija Medicine is the structurally anomalous option in the Spanish private medicine landscape: the complete absence of a written academic knowledge exam creates a genuinely different admission pathway. For candidates who have been preparing for biology and chemistry exams at other schools but are concerned about their exam-day performance on those subjects, Nebrija removes that variable entirely — replacing it with a psychometric test and English assessment. This is not a workaround; it is the official model.

The risk profile, however, is asymmetric. The upside is real: low current competitiveness, 90 seats, a differentiated selection model, early clinical training, and a well-resourced university backing. The downside is equally real: no MIR data, unconfirmed WFME status, psychometric test opacity, and a rural campus requiring daily commuting. Whether this trade-off is strategically sound depends entirely on the individual student's profile, geographic situation, and international career aspirations.

## Final Takeaway

Nebrija Medicine is best positioned as a strategic safety option in a broader private medicine application portfolio — particularly for students whose profile aligns with psychometric-based selection and who are not primarily relying on science exam performance. Its 2024-2025 inception, 90 seats and absence of a knowledge exam make it currently accessible; the Becas de Excelencia (up to 5 awards, 50–80% of monthly fees) offer meaningful financial relief for high-performing students with limited family income. The program sits in the lower tier of confidence for established-school comparisons (behind UNAV, UIC, UAX, CEU San Pablo) primarily due to the absence of MIR data and unconfirmed WFME status — but it fills a genuine gap in the market for candidates who need a viable, properly accredited Spanish private medicine degree that does not require science exam preparation.

**Can I use a Cambridge or IELTS certificate instead of sitting the English test?**
Yes, if the certificate is recognised by ACLES (Asociación de Centros de Lenguas en la Enseñanza Superior). A valid ACLES-recognized certificate submitted with the candidacy application exempts the candidate from the onsite English level test.

**What grades do I need to be admitted?**
No explicit minimum grade for admission is published. The required bachillerato track is Ciencias de la Salud or Ciencias. The ≥9.0 nota media + ≥12.6 EVAU threshold applies only to the Becas de Excelencia scholarship — not to the admission process itself.

**Is the campus in Madrid city?**
No. The Medicine degree is taught at the Campus de Ciencias de la Vida en La Berzosa, approximately 35–40 km northwest of Madrid city center. Some admission tests may be conducted at Campus Princesa in central Madrid.

**Is Nebrija Medicine WFME accredited?**
PENDING_CONFIRMATION. WFME accreditation has not been confirmed. Candidates with career plans in the USA, Canada, Australia or other WFME-requiring jurisdictions must confirm this status directly with the university before committing.

**How competitive is admission compared to other private medicine schools in Spain?**
Currently low. The program entered its third cohort in 2026-2027. No cut-off score data is published. The no-knowledge-exam model reduces the directly comparable preparation overlap with other schools. This competitiveness level could change as the program matures and attracts more applicants.

**Can I apply for a scholarship?**
Yes, if both academic and economic criteria are met: nota media ≥9.0 in Year 2 Bachillerato, EvAU ≥12.6 pts in June, and documented family income within the defined thresholds (applicable to Spanish tax residents only). Up to 5 scholarships are available per intake, covering 50–80% of monthly tuition fees.

**Is the reservation deposit refundable?**
Only under two conditions: if the candidate fails bachillerato/EvAU in the June call, or in cases of serious documented medical conditions preventing enrolment. In all other circumstances, the 3,000 EUR deposit is non-refundable.

**Are the FIU and Ponce exchange programs guaranteed?**
No. Exchange availability is ANNUAL_UPDATE_REQUIRED. The partnerships exist and are listed on the Medicine degree page, but available places per year and individual eligibility conditions are not published centrally. Confirm with the Departamento de Programas Internacionales before each intake.

**Is there an interview as part of the Medicine admission process?**
No. Although general Nebrija admissions information for other degrees references an entrevista personal, this does not appear in the published Medicine-specific admission procedure. The Medicine process consists of document submission, onsite psychometric test, and onsite English test.
