# Centro Universitario HM Hospitales de Ciencias de la Salud de la UCJC (CUHMED)

## Overview

CUHMED is the academic result of a formal partnership between Grupo HM Hospitales — the largest private hospital network in Spain by bed count — and Universidad Camilo José Cela (UCJC), itself part of the SEK Education Group. The arrangement converts every HM Hospitales facility into a university hospital, creating what is marketed as the broadest hospital-integrated Medicine degree in the country.

The program launched its first cohort in 2023. For 2026-27 intake, Years 1 through 3 have already completed their first full cycle; Year 4 onwards has not yet run. Degree duration is six years, 360 ECTS, taught entirely in Spanish, presencial. Primary teaching campus is HM Montepríncipe (Boadilla del Monte, Madrid); the admissions exam and laboratory sciences are hosted at Campus Villafranca del Castillo.

Admission uses a straightforward three-component weighted model: 60% Y1 Bachillerato academic record, 30% in-person Biology and Chemistry MCQ exam, 10% competency assessment. EBAU/EvAU scores play no role. This makes CUHMED particularly accessible for students with strong secondary records who did not sit or score well on the EBAU.

At 23,220 EUR per year (Y1, new students), this is one of the higher-cost Medicine programs in Spain. The program's core strategic proposition is structured clinical immersion from the earliest years, access to the full HM Hospitales network, and a formal MIR tutoring agreement with CTO (claimed to be unique in Spain).

## Program Options

CUHMED offers a single degree track in Medicine: 6-year presencial program in Spanish. There is no bilingual variant, no online modality, and no separate campus track. The degree is formally validated under UCJC's institutional accreditation.

Two physical sites are involved: the research and lab sciences building at Campus Villafranca del Castillo (2,500 m²), where the admissions exam is also held, and HM Montepríncipe in Boadilla del Monte, which hosts a dedicated 6,000 m² teaching pavilion for the clinical-phase years. Students in Years 1–2 split activity between both sites; from Year 3 onwards the HM Montepríncipe campus becomes the primary location.

## Program Structure

The curriculum spans six years of 60 ECTS each, for a total of 360 ECTS. The institutional credit distribution is: Formación básica 90 ECTS, Obligatorias 204 ECTS, Optativas 6 ECTS, Prácticas externas 54 ECTS, TFG 6 ECTS.

The program can be divided into three functional phases:

**Phase 1 — Basic sciences (Years 1–2):** Core biomedical foundations. Anatomy, Biochemistry, Cell Biology, Physiology, Immunology, Microbiology, and the morphological systems approach characteristic of the HM model (respiratory/cardiovascular, digestive/nefrourinary, nervous system). Year 2 introduces the first clinical communication module (5 ECTS, annual).

**Phase 2 — Pathophysiology and clinical introduction (Years 3–4):** Progressive integration of organ-based pathophysiology with clinical application. Pharmacology (basic and clinical), Anatomía Patológica, Radiology, Bioethics, and the first structured Práctica Clínica rotations (5 ECTS in Y3, 8 ECTS in Y4). Research methodology thread runs across all three years (Biostatistics Y2, Investigación básica-traslacional Y3, Proyecto de investigación Y4).

**Phase 3 — Clinical specialties and intensive practice (Years 5–6):** Full specialty coverage including Locomotor, Pediatrics (I+II), Psychiatry, Infectious Disease, Oncology/Hematology, Preventive Medicine, Forensic Medicine, Geriatrics, and Intensive Care. Year 5 includes Práctica Clínica III (8 ECTS). Year 6 is dominated by Práctica Clínica IV (33 ECTS) plus TFG (6 ECTS), representing a near-full-year hospital immersion.

The only listed elective is a 3 ECTS module in Y3 (Fundamentos de la Inteligencia Artificial en atención médica) and a 3 ECTS generic optativa slot in Y4.

## Teaching Methodology

CUHMED's pedagogical framework is defined by three claims: hospital integration from the first semester, a technology-enhanced anatomy laboratory (Anatomage Table — one of few in Spain), and a structured research thread spanning Years 2 through 4.

The Anatomage Table enables three-dimensional interactive anatomy visualization at cadaver scale, replacing or supplementing traditional dissection. Its practical impact on anatomical preparation is considered significant in institutions that have deployed it.

From Y1, students may access HM Hospitals through the "Minijobs" program, which offers paid and structured contact with hospital departments — not clinical practice in the formal sense, but exposure to real healthcare operations, patient communication, and multidisciplinary teams.

CUHMED holds a formal tutoring agreement with CTO (Centro de Técnicas de Ortopedia, a major Spanish MIR preparation organization). The program markets this as a structured MIR-aligned tutoring system integrated into the degree, described as unique nationally. The contractual and operational details of this agreement have not been independently published.

The faculty is composed almost exclusively of active clinicians from HM Hospitales. Key professors include: Dr. José Barberán López (Decano, Jefe de Medicina Interna HM Montepríncipe), Dr. Emilio de Vicente López (Jefe de Cirugía General HM Sanchinarro), Dr. Antonio Cubillo Gracián (Jefe CIOCC HM Hospitals), and Dr. José Obeso (Catedrático de Neurología, Director HM CINAC).

## Clinical Training

Clinical training is embedded progressively across the six years, anchored entirely in the HM Hospitales network.

**Year 2:** Introducción a la práctica Clínica — Comunicación con el paciente (5 ECTS, annual). First structured contact with hospital environment.

**Year 3:** Práctica Clínica I (5 ECTS). Initial supervised clinical rotations.

**Year 4:** Práctica Clínica II (8 ECTS). Extended rotations across medical and surgical specialties.

**Year 5:** Práctica Clínica III (8 ECTS). Advanced specialty rotations.

**Year 6:** Práctica Clínica IV (33 ECTS). Near-full-year immersion across hospital departments — equivalent in scope to an early MIR rotation cycle.

The declared hospital network includes (ANNUAL_UPDATE_REQUIRED): HM Montepríncipe (primary), HM Torrelodones, HM Sanchinarro, HM Puerta del Sur, HM Vallés, HM Rivas, HM Nuevo Belén, plus multiple policlínicos and Centros de Salud in the Comunidad de Madrid. Forensic training, where required, is coordinated through external institutions.

The primary site for clinical teaching (HM Montepríncipe) is a tertiary general hospital with 2,000+ beds across the network. The scale and diversity of cases across the full HM system is the program's principal practical advantage.

## International Recognition and Opportunities

CUHMED operates under UCJC's institutional ANECA accreditation. The degree is titled under the Spanish Ministry of Education's official Medicine framework and is recognized for MIR access (Médico Interno Residente) once graduates complete the national qualifying exam.

**WFME accreditation:** PENDING_CONFIRMATION. Students who intend to practice outside Spain or pursue postgraduate training in countries that require WFME-accredited degrees must verify this directly with CUHMED before enrolling.

**European recognition:** As an ANECA-accredited Spanish degree within the EU, the Medicine title is eligible for mutual recognition across EU member states under Directive 2005/36/EC, subject to individual country procedures.

**QS Stars:** 4/5 institutional rating.

## Admission Model

CUHMED uses a weighted three-component model with no threshold eliminations and no interview. The total score is the weighted sum of three assessments:

- **60% — Expediente académico:** The academic record from Y1 Bachillerato (first year of upper secondary) only. Y2 Bachillerato grades and EBAU/EvAU scores are excluded from the formula. This component rewards consistent strong performance in Y11 subjects, independently of performance in the university entrance exam.

- **30% — Prueba específica:** An in-person multiple-choice exam covering Biology and Chemistry, held at Campus Villafranca del Castillo. Duration approximately 2 hours. The exact syllabus scope and negative marking policy are PENDING_CONFIRMATION (no model exam is published). Biology and Chemistry are the only subjects tested; Physics and Mathematics are not included.

- **10% — Evaluación de competencias:** An in-person assessment of transversal competencies, entrepreneurship capacity, and digital skills. Integrated into the same exam session. Format details are PENDING_CONFIRMATION.

No interview. No English test. No ISAT. No bonus points for additional certifications.

The non-EBAU structure is a deliberate design choice: it opens access to students with strong continuous academic records who may not have optimized for the EBAU, including international students, students from CFGS backgrounds, and Spanish students who performed better in Y1 than in the standardized national exam.

## Admission Process

1. **Register and pay the process fee (150 EUR, non-refundable).** This must be completed before November 7 of the year prior to the target intake (i.e., before November 7, 2025 for September 2026 intake). Payment secures the candidate's slot in the admissions exam. ANNUAL_UPDATE_REQUIRED.

2. **Submit academic documentation.** Upload academic transcript (Y1 Bachillerato) and required supporting documents through the UCJC academic portal. Status can be tracked online.

3. **Attend the admissions exam in person.** Upon confirmed registration, the university sends an exam appointment by email. The exam is held at Campus Villafranca del Castillo and covers the Biology/Chemistry MCQ plus the competency evaluation in the same session (~2 hours total).

4. **Await results and confirm enrollment.** Upon admission, pay the apertura de expediente (300 EUR, new students only), followed by the formal matriculation fee (3,000 EUR) and begin monthly fee payments.

## Admission Score Distribution

The 60/30/10 weighting structure places significant emphasis on the secondary academic record, making this a relatively accessible model for strong academic performers who struggled on the EBAU or who chose not to optimize their exam preparation for specific EBAU subject combinations.

**Y1 Bachillerato (60%):** The dominant factor. A student with a 9.0 average in Y1 enters the formula at 5.4 of 6.0 from this component alone, creating a strong base before the exam. Students with grades below 8.0 face a structural disadvantage that the exam and competency components cannot fully compensate.

**Biology + Chemistry MCQ (30%):** This is the preparable differentiator. Because no model exam or detailed syllabus is published, preparation requires broad command of both Biology and Chemistry at Bachillerato level. Students who have focused preparation specifically on these two subjects — even without comprehensive EBAU preparation — have a realistic competitive advantage here.

**Evaluación de competencias (10%):** A limited-weight component. Its impact on final ranking is modest in practice, but it remains a non-trivial differentiator when scores cluster. Preparation strategies are difficult to define without a published framework.

**Score clustering:** Given the absence of published cut-off scores and the relatively small contribution of the competency component, the practical selection pressure falls almost entirely on the Y1 Bachillerato grade and the quality of Biology/Chemistry preparation.

## Strategic Fit

### Good Fit For

- Students with strong Y1 Bachillerato records (≥8.5) and solid Biology and Chemistry preparation who did not perform well on the EBAU or did not prioritize EBAU optimization.
- Madrid-based or Madrid-area students for whom proximity to the HM hospital network and the western suburban campuses is practical.
- Students who prioritize early and continuous hospital exposure over a traditional pre-clinical/clinical phased structure.
- Students who value the Anatomage Table and CTO tutoring integration as concrete MIR preparation features.
- International students or students from non-EBAU tracks (CFGS, international Bachillerato) for whom the no-EBAU admission model removes a major structural barrier.

### Less Suitable For

- Students seeking the lowest-cost Medicine option; at 23,220 EUR/year this program sits at the upper end of Spanish private Medicine pricing.
- Students who require confirmed WFME accreditation before enrollment — this status for CUHMED specifically is unconfirmed at this file's last update.
- Students who prefer a campus environment well-integrated with urban public transport; both primary campuses are in the suburban west of Madrid and require private transport.
- Students seeking an established program with published MIR outcomes — the first graduating cohort is not expected until 2029.

## Risks and Considerations

**Program maturity:** CUHMED launched its first year in 2023. As of the 2026-27 intake, only Years 1–3 have completed their first full run. No student has graduated, no MIR data exists, and the program's long-term execution of Years 4–6 has not yet been independently observed. The curriculum for these years is published and formally accredited, but operational quality in the clinical-heavy years remains unverified.

**WFME accreditation:** Not confirmed for the CUHMED degree specifically. For students intending to practice in countries that require WFME-accredited Spanish degrees (certain non-EU countries), this must be resolved before enrollment.

**Hospital network concentration:** The entire clinical training model depends on Grupo HM Hospitales. Any structural, financial, or operational change to the HM-UCJC partnership would directly affect the clinical training component. There is no secondary hospital network.

**Seats not disclosed:** CUHMED does not publish its annual intake capacity. This creates uncertainty about actual competitiveness and selectivity.

**Cost exposure:** At 23,220 EUR/year without a firm multi-year pricing guarantee, total six-year academic cost exposure is approximately 130,000–140,000 EUR before living costs. Scholarship coverage is partial and uncertain; the Medicine-specific scholarship program details are not publicly available.

**Exam syllabus opacity:** The Biology/Chemistry admissions exam has no published model, no syllabus specification, and no negative marking policy. This makes targeted preparation difficult and introduces variability risk for well-prepared candidates who may face unexpected question formats.

## Key Insight

CUHMED's differentiating proposition is structural, not aspirational: embedding a university degree inside an active hospital network from day one is a genuine architectural change to how Medicine is taught, not a marketing overlay on a conventional program. The 54 ECTS of Prácticas Externas (versus the national minimum of 40) and the 33-ECTS Year 6 immersion block are indicators that the clinical integration is built into the curriculum design, not added as enrichment.

The strategic risk is the inverse of the proposition's strength: the program's quality depends entirely on HM Hospitales remaining a stable, functional clinical partner and on UCJC managing the academic side of a hospital-university model that has no long-term precedent in Spain. Students enrolling in 2026 are, in effect, enrolling in an institution whose actual graduation and MIR outcomes will not be known until 2029.

For students who have clarity on this trade-off and who have strong Biology/Chemistry preparation and a solid Y1 Bachillerato grade, CUHMED offers one of the most distinctive clinical learning environments available in private Medicine education in Spain.

## Final Takeaway

CUHMED is a credible, well-resourced option for Madrid-area students targeting private Medicine with strong Y1 Bachillerato records and Biology/Chemistry preparation. The no-EBAU admission model, the hospital-integrated curriculum, the Anatomage Table, and the CTO tutoring arrangement are concrete differentiators.

The constraints are equally concrete: high annual cost (~23,220 EUR), unconfirmed WFME status, no graduate cohort, and the operational dependence on a single hospital group. Students who need price certainty, WFME confirmation, or proof of MIR outcomes before committing should wait or consider parallel applications to more established programs. Students comfortable with the program's stage of development and attracted by its hospital-first model will find it among the more thoughtfully designed private Medicine degrees in Spain.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does CUHMED require the EBAU/EvAU for admission?**
No. The EBAU score plays no role in the admission formula. Selection is based entirely on Y1 Bachillerato grade (60%), an in-person Biology/Chemistry MCQ exam (30%), and a competency assessment (10%).

**What subjects are tested in the admissions exam?**
Biology and Chemistry, in a multiple-choice format. Duration is approximately 2 hours. The exact syllabus scope is PENDING_CONFIRMATION; no model exam is published.

**What is the 150 EUR fee paid before November 7?**
It is a non-refundable process fee that grants access to the admissions exam. It does not guarantee admission; it registers the candidate to sit the exam. The 150 EUR is not applied toward tuition if admitted.

**Is CUHMED WFME accredited?**
WFME accreditation for the CUHMED Medicine degree specifically is PENDING_CONFIRMATION. UCJC has institutional ANECA recognition, and the degree is valid for MIR in Spain and for EU mutual recognition. Students planning to practice in countries with WFME requirements should verify this before enrolling.

**What is the total six-year estimated cost?**
Based on published 2026-27 pricing, Year 1 costs 23,220 EUR in academic fees. Subsequent years are priced at the rate per ECTS in force at the time of enrollment (332 EUR/ECTS for 2026-27). A six-year estimate at current rates would be approximately 120,000–140,000 EUR in academic fees, before living costs and supplementary charges. ANNUAL_UPDATE_REQUIRED.

**Are there scholarships available for Medicine specifically?**
UCJC has a dedicated Programa de Becas Grado en Medicina. Details of amounts and criteria are PENDING_CONFIRMATION. National government scholarships (Ministerio, Comunidad de Madrid Excelencia, Navarra, País Vasco) are also applicable with varying discount percentages on tuition.

**Does CUHMED have a clinical training agreement with any residency programs?**
Clinical training takes place entirely within Grupo HM Hospitales, which is a major private network. However, access to MIR positions in HM Hospitals after graduation follows the standard national MIR process and is not guaranteed. The CTO tutoring agreement supports MIR preparation within the degree but does not constitute a placement guarantee.

**What campus do students primarily use?**
Years 1–2: mix of Campus Villafranca del Castillo (labs, sciences) and HM Montepríncipe. Years 3–6: primarily HM Montepríncipe in Boadilla del Monte. The admissions exam is always held at Campus Villafranca del Castillo.
