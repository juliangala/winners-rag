# Universidad Europea de Canarias (UEC) — Grado en Medicina

## Overview

Universidad Europea de Canarias (UEC) is a private university in La Orotava, Tenerife, part of the broader Universidad Europea group (owned by Permira). Its Medicine degree is the only private Medicine program in the Canary Islands, making it the sole private option for students based in the archipelago and a unique geographic proposition for students seeking an island setting.

The program launches its inaugural cohort in September 2026 with 50 seats. The six-year, 360-ECTS degree is taught primarily in Spanish with selected subjects in English (Years 2–4). Clinical training begins in Year 3 through Grupo Hospiten (Hospiten Bellevue, Rambla, and Sur — all university hospitals in Tenerife), supported by a hospital simulado on campus.

The admissions exam is conducted virtually, which reduces geographic and logistical barriers for applicants outside Tenerife and international candidates. Non-native Spanish speakers must pass an integrated B2 Spanish language test as part of the admissions exam; failure to reach B2 results in automatic disqualification.

With only 50 seats for an inaugural cohort and a virtual exam format, UEC Medicine is strategically positioned as an accessible entry point into private Medicine in Spain for students with a reasonable academic profile who may find more competitive programs inaccessible.

## Program Options

UEC offers a single Medicine degree track: 6-year presencial program, primarily in Spanish, with four subjects taught in English (Comunicación y divulgación científica in Y2, Medicina basada en la evidencia y la tecnología in Y2, Gestión sanitaria sostenible in Y3, and Medicina preventiva y salud pública in Y4). There is no bilingual track, no online variant, and no separate campus or degree pathway.

The program is housed in the Facultad de Ciencias de la Salud in La Orotava, Tenerife. The campus includes a dedicated hospital simulado — a multi-space simulation facility with advanced equipment aligned to each Medicine subject — used for pre-clinical skills development before students begin hospital rotations in Year 3.

## Program Structure

The curriculum spans six years of 60 ECTS each (360 ECTS total). The credit distribution is: Formación básica 90 ECTS, Obligatorias 201 ECTS, Optativas 9 ECTS, Prácticas Asistenciales Externas (PAE) 54 ECTS, TFG 6 ECTS.

The curriculum uses an integrated systems-based approach rather than a discipline-by-discipline structure. Major organ systems are covered holistically across years rather than as separate anatomy/physiology/pathology courses:

**Years 1–2 — Basic biomedical sciences:** Systems anatomy and physiology (cardiovascular, locomotor, nervous system, respiratory, gastrointestinal, endocrine/reproductive), biochemistry, cell biology, genetics, biostatistics/epidemiology, and foundations of clinical methodology. Year 2 includes an "Introducción preclínica" module (6 ECTS) as a bridge toward clinical competencies.

**Year 3 — Clinical integration:** Pathophysiology and semiología (12 ECTS integrated block), pharmacology fundamentals, surgery basics, Anatomy Patológica, and the first PAE block (Medicina familiar, 3 ECTS in Atención Primaria). Three English-language sessions across Years 2–4 develop academic communication skills and evidence-based medicine.

**Years 4–5 — Clinical specialties:** Systematic coverage of all major clinical specialties: cardiovascular, digestive, respiratory, endocrinology, neurology, infectious diseases, hematology, pediatrics, psychiatry, gynecology/obstetrics, dermatology, ophthalmology, geriatrics, oncology, emergency medicine, and more.

**Year 6 — Clinical rotations (PAE, 51 ECTS) + TFG (6 ECTS) + Genómica clínica (3 ECTS):** Near-full-year hospital immersion across 10 defined rotations, covering medicine interna, urgencias, psychiatry, general surgery, primary care, pediatrics, two blocks of medical specialties, surgical specialties, and gynecology/obstetrics.

Electives (3 ECTS per year): In Years 2, 3, and 5, students choose one elective from two options per year. Available electives include IA in health (Y2), ECG interpretation (Y3), ultrasound (Y5), and telemedicine/digital health (Y5).

A distinctive research thread runs across Years 1–5: biostatistics (Y1), evidence-based medicine (Y2), precision medicine (Y3), and "Inquietud investigadora" (Y5, 6 ECTS research initiative). The final year curriculum includes a genomics module covering clinical application of basic sciences.

## Teaching Methodology

UEC's pedagogical model for Medicine is described as experiential, competency-based, and integrating high-fidelity simulation with real clinical environments. The hospital simulado provides dedicated simulation spaces equipped for each academic subject, with rotations organized in sub-groups to maximize instructional quality. This allows progressive skill development before students enter live patient environments.

The curriculum integrates evidence-based practice throughout: the Year 2 subject "Medicina basada en la evidencia y la tecnología" (6 ECTS, English) explicitly trains students in applying research evidence to clinical decision-making, a skill with direct MIR relevance. "Herramientas de investigación: bioestadística y epidemiología" in Year 1 grounds students in quantitative methodology from the start.

The English-language subjects embedded across Years 2–4 are not language-learning courses but are subject-matter taught in English — developing academic English in a medical context without requiring a bilingual track. This is a strategic differentiator for internationally-oriented students.

The Year 6 clinical rotation block (51 ECTS PAE) covers 10 structured rotation areas with explicit specialty definitions, providing systematic breadth across all primary MIR specialties.

## Clinical Training

Clinical training is organized in two phases. A first clinical contact occurs in Year 3 through a Medicina familiar rotation (3 ECTS PAE) in Atención Primaria centers. Full hospital rotations begin in Year 6 across Grupo Hospiten's university hospital network in Tenerife.

**Year 3 — Atención Primaria (3 ECTS PAE):** Initial supervised contact with primary care settings.

**Year 6 — Full clinical rotations (51 ECTS PAE) across:**
- Medicina interna (6 ECTS)
- Urgencias y emergencias (4 ECTS)
- Psiquiatría (4 ECTS)
- Cirugía general (5 ECTS)
- Atención primaria (5 ECTS)
- Pediatría y neonatología (6 ECTS)
- Especialidades médicas I (6 ECTS)
- Especialidades quirúrgicas (3 ECTS)
- Ginecología y obstetricia (6 ECTS)
- Especialidades médicas II (6 ECTS)

**Hospital network (ANNUAL_UPDATE_REQUIRED):** Hospital Universitario Hospiten Bellevue, Hospital Universitario Hospiten Rambla, Hospital Universitario Hospiten Sur — all three are university hospitals in Tenerife belonging to Grupo Hospiten, a network with over 50 years of activity, 20 hospitals across 5 countries.

The entire clinical training network is confined to Tenerife and to a single hospital group. This is both a structural limitation (case diversity depends on one network) and a practical simplification (no relocation required, consistent protocols).

## Admission Model

The UEC Canarias Medicine admissions process operates through a virtual exam, making it geographically accessible to candidates nationwide and internationally without requiring travel to Tenerife for the selection stage.

The admissions sequence is: candidacy submission → virtual exam → admission decision → reservation (5 working days) → documentation.

## Accommodation and Cost of Living

La Orotava is a historic municipality in the interior of Tenerife, situated on the slopes of Teide in the Orotava Valley. The town itself is approximately 15–20 km from Santa Cruz de Tenerife (the capital) and has no direct metro connection. Local transport options are mainly bus (guagua) and private vehicle.

The Canary Islands generally offer a lower cost of living than mainland Spain, particularly in non-tourist interior municipalities. Rough estimate for a student living independently in La Orotava: 700–950 EUR/month excluding tuition (accommodation, food, transport, personal), significantly lower than Madrid or Barcelona. Students arriving from mainland Spain or abroad may face higher initial setup costs.

Island-specific considerations: flights between Tenerife and the Spanish mainland are frequent but add up for students who travel home regularly; car ownership or rental is practical for independence. Social and extracurricular options are more limited than in major mainland cities.

## Strategic Fit

### Good Fit For

- Students from the Canary Islands who want to study Medicine locally and remain connected to the island healthcare ecosystem.
- International students or students from non-mainland Spain backgrounds for whom the virtual exam format significantly reduces logistical barriers.
- Students with solid but not highly competitive academic profiles who want a private Medicine program with lower initial selectivity.
- Students who value early English-language integration within a predominantly Spanish-taught program.
- Students attracted by lower living costs in Tenerife compared to major mainland Spanish cities.

### Less Suitable For

- Students who require confirmed WFME accreditation before enrollment for international practice.
- Students who need verified MIR outcomes before choosing a program — none exist for this inaugural program.
- Students who prefer a major urban setting with broad cultural, professional networking, and extracurricular options.
- Students for whom pricing transparency is essential before applying — fees were not published at the time of this file's creation.
- Students with strong academic profiles who would be competitive at more established private programs where infrastructure and track records are proven.

## Risks and Considerations

**Inaugural cohort:** No student has graduated, no MIR results exist, and the operational quality of the full 6-year program — including Years 3–6 clinical training — has not yet been executed. All stated features are aspirational pending actual delivery.

**WFME and ANECA status:** Both are unconfirmed for this specific program as of this file's update. Students intending to practice internationally or requiring Spanish degree recognition in third countries must verify these before enrolling.

**Single hospital group dependency:** All clinical training relies on Grupo Hospiten in Tenerife. Any change in the UEC–Hospiten partnership would directly affect clinical placement availability and quality.

**Island geography constraints:** The Canary Islands are geographically isolated. Students who choose UEC are committing to spending 6 years in Tenerife. Career networking, MIR course access, and extracurricular medical activity are primarily limited to the island unless students invest in mainland travel.

**Pricing opacity:** The absence of published tuition figures makes budget planning impossible without direct inquiry. This is a practical obstacle in the comparison stage for prospective students.

**Admissions model opacity:** The exact exam components, weights, and scoring methodology are not publicly available, which prevents precise preparation targeting.

## Key Insight

UEC Canarias fills a genuine geographic gap: there is no other private Medicine program in the Canary Islands. For students from the archipelago, this eliminates the need to relocate to mainland Spain, with all the personal and financial costs that entails. For international students, the virtual exam format provides access to a formally valid Spanish Medicine degree without requiring a physical visit to Spain at the application stage.

The program's weakness is structural: being first in its geography does not compensate for being unproven. Students enrolling in 2026 are making a six-year commitment to a program with no graduate outcomes, unconfirmed international accreditation, and a single clinical partner. The low initial selectivity, while operationally convenient, also reflects the uncertainty that surrounds a new program — not a deliberate accessibility policy.

For students who are specifically anchored to the Canary Islands or who have exhausted more established options, UEC Medicine is a viable path. For students with options, it should be a backup rather than a first choice until the program demonstrates outcomes across multiple cohorts.

## Final Takeaway

UEC Canarias' Medicine degree is strategically interesting as a geographic monopoly and logistically accessible due to its virtual exam. For the right student profile — Canary Islands-based, internationally mobile, or seeking a lower-cost Spanish island setting — it makes practical sense. The core risks (inaugural cohort, unconfirmed accreditations, pricing opacity, clinical monoculture on Tenerife) require careful evaluation before committing. Students who can tolerate these uncertainties in exchange for island-based or remotely-accessible Medicine education will find UEC a structurally sound program with a well-designed curriculum.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is UEC Canarias the only private Medicine program in the Canary Islands?**
Yes. As of the 2026-27 intake, it is the only private Medicine degree in the archipelago.

**Do I need to travel to Tenerife to sit the admissions exam?**
No. The admissions exam is conducted virtually. Candidates connect on a confirmed date remotely.

**Is B2 Spanish required for admission?**
Yes, for non-native Spanish speakers. A Spanish language test is integrated into the admissions exam. Failure to reach B2 results in automatic disqualification, regardless of other scores.

**When do clinical rotations begin?**
Clinical hospital rotations begin in Year 6. The first supervised clinical contact occurs in Year 3 through a Medicina familiar module (3 ECTS) in Atención Primaria.

**Which hospitals will I train in?**
Clinical training takes place in the three university hospitals of Grupo Hospiten in Tenerife: Hospital Universitario Hospiten Bellevue, Hospital Universitario Hospiten Rambla, and Hospital Universitario Hospiten Sur. ANNUAL_UPDATE_REQUIRED.
