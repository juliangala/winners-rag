# Universidad de Deusto — Medicine

## Overview

The Grado en Medicina at Universidad de Deusto is a six-year, 360 ECTS programme based on the Bilbao campus of one of Spain's most historically prestigious private universities. Deusto is a Jesuit institution with over 135 years of history and strong academic credentials across law, business, engineering, and social sciences — its Medicine degree, launched more recently, brings this institutional heritage into the health sciences.

The programme is built around two defining pedagogical pillars: Problem-Based Learning (PBL/ABP methodology) — through which students learn via hypothesis testing applied to real clinical cases — and person-centred medicine, which emphasises treating the individual rather than the disease and integrates ethics, bioethics, and communication throughout all six years.

A structural differentiator is hospital exposure from Year 1: pre-clinical laboratory practice and simulation exercises begin immediately, and full clinical rotations with real patients run from Year 3 onwards. Deusto's DeustoBio facility houses a Virtual Hospital for simulation, with a physiology and biomechanics laboratory, skills rooms, consulting rooms, ward room, ICU, operating theatre, and debriefing rooms — a purpose-built clinical training environment within the campus.

With 80 places, the programme is one of the smaller cohorts among Spanish private Medicine programmes, which directly affects the competitiveness of admission. The admission model is academic-record-based with a potential selective interview, without a proprietary academic exam — a structurally different approach from most comparable programmes.

Teaching is delivered in Spanish, English, and optionally Basque, with the Basque-language track offering sufficient credits for official C1 Basque language profile recognition — a distinctive feature unique to this Basque Country programme.

## Program Options

Deusto offers a single Medicine degree programme on the Bilbao campus. There is no separate Spanish-only track or a fully English-taught track — the programme is multilingual by design, with subjects delivered in Spanish, some in English, and an optional Basque-language stream.

The **optional Basque-language (Euskara) track** enables students to accumulate sufficient credits for official recognition of the C1 Basque language profile — a credential with practical value for medical practice in the public healthcare system of the Basque Country (Osakidetza), where Basque-language capacity is assessed in professional selectivity processes.

The **Honors Programme** is available for high-achieving students seeking advanced academic challenge beyond the standard curriculum — details PENDING_CONFIRMATION.

A **mandatory 2-month international clinical placement** in 4th year is embedded in the degree structure, not an optional Erasmus exchange — this distinguishes Deusto from programmes where international mobility is purely elective.

## Program Structure

The programme spans six years across twelve semesters, structured around the PBL methodology and progressive clinical integration.

**Years 1–2 — Foundation Phase:** Pre-clinical laboratory practice with simulation exercises and patient simulation in the Virtual Hospital begin immediately from Year 1. The curriculum covers foundational biomedical sciences through PBL-based small group work facilitated by academic tutors. Content covers the standard biomedical science base (anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, histology, microbiology, pathology, pharmacology) delivered through the PBL case methodology rather than traditional lecture-based teaching.

**Years 3–6 — Clinical Phase:** From Year 3, students conduct clinical practice with real patients across Deusto's affiliated hospital network. Year 4 includes a mandatory 2-month clinical placement abroad (international mobility). Year 6 includes the Undergraduate Final Year Project (Trabajo Final de Grado de Pregrado, 18 ECTS) — a project associated with clinical practice as the principal Year 6 deliverable.

**Compulsory clinical internships:** 42 ECTS of mandatory external placements (prácticas obligatorias externas) are embedded in the programme — ANNUAL_UPDATE_REQUIRED for specific year-by-year distribution.

**Total credit structure:** 360 ECTS over 6 years. Subject-by-subject breakdown available via the official study plan (plan de estudios) at deusto.es.

## Teaching Methodology

Deusto's pedagogical identity is centred on **Problem-Based Learning (ABP — Aprendizaje Basado en Problemas)**, in which most theoretical content is delivered in small groups led by a teacher-facilitator rather than in large lectures. Students learn through hypothesis testing applied to clinical cases — building reasoning and diagnostic skills from the beginning of the degree rather than deferring clinical thinking to later years.

**Person-centred medicine** is the philosophical frame: the curriculum is designed around treating individuals, not diseases. Communication skills, empathetic engagement with patients, and bioethics are integrated throughout rather than isolated in dedicated modules.

**Tutoring system:** Each student has an assigned tutor providing academic, personal, and professional guidance throughout the degree. The University Orientation Service (SOU — Servicio de Orientación Universitaria) provides complementary support.

**Interprofessional collaboration:** The Jesuit educational model incorporates a Human Training in Values Module (Módulo de Formación Humana en Valores) as part of the university-wide academic framework, which applies to Medicine students.

The combination of PBL methodology, small-group learning, early simulation, and integrated clinical exposure from Year 1 creates a pedagogical environment structured around active learning rather than passive content transmission.

## Clinical Training

Clinical exposure at Deusto is structured in two distinct phases:

**Pre-clinical phase (Years 1–2):** Laboratory practice, patient interaction, and simulation exercises at the DeustoBio facility on the Bilbao campus. The Virtual Hospital enables simulation practice across a full range of clinical environments — skills rooms, consulting rooms, ward room, ICU, and operating theatre — with dedicated debriefing rooms for post-simulation analysis.

**Clinical phase (Years 3–6):** Full clinical rotations with real patients at affiliated hospitals and healthcare centres in the Bilbao metropolitan area. Clinical specialties covered across this phase align with the standard Medicine curriculum: internal medicine, surgery, paediatrics, gynaecology and obstetrics, psychiatry, neurology, and primary care — among others.

**International clinical placement (Year 4):** A mandatory 2-month clinical placement abroad is embedded in Year 4, providing students with exposure to a different healthcare system and cultural environment as a formal part of the degree.

## Admission Model

Deusto's admission model is distinctly different from the majority of private Medicine programmes in Spain: **no proprietary academic exam is required**. Selection is based primarily on the academic transcript, with language accreditation as an additional criterion in applicable cases, and a selective personal interview that the admissions committee may call at its discretion.

This creates a model that is **academic-record-heavy and qualitative** rather than test-score-competitive. Candidates cannot compensate for a weaker academic record through strong exam performance — the record is both the primary and dominant factor.

The official admissions criteria document states that the committee evaluates: (1) the academic transcript (expediente académico), and (2) language accreditation in cases where it is a requirement. The committee reserves the right to call candidates for a personal interview or to request additional documentation.

The absence of a published weighting formula means the admission decision process has lower transparency than weighted composite models used by other programmes. Admission outcomes are less predictable for borderline candidates, and the selective interview introduces a qualitative judgment dimension.

For 80 available places with strong national and Basque Country interest, the effective competition for places is high. Candidates without near-competitive academic records face structural disadvantage in this model.

## Admission Score Distribution

Deusto's model does not publish a weighted scoring formula. The primary evaluation axis is the academic transcript in its entirety — not only the final grade but the quality and consistency of performance across secondary education. Language accreditation adds a qualification dimension in applicable cases.

**Strategic reading:** Candidates applying to Deusto Medicine should present the strongest possible academic transcript. Unlike models where a strong aptitude test can compensate for moderate grades, Deusto's approach gives no mechanism for offsetting a weaker record. The holistic, committee-based decision process means that candidates near the admission threshold face higher outcome uncertainty.

The selective interview, if called, serves as a qualitative gate — likely designed to assess motivation, communication, interpersonal aptitude, and alignment with Deusto's person-centred medicine philosophy. No published format or scoring rubric is available; preparation should focus on articulating genuine medical vocation, awareness of the PBL methodology, and the humanistic dimension of the Jesuit educational tradition.

With 80 places and no published minimum grade, the effective competitive bar is set by the pool of applicants each cycle. Given Deusto's strong institutional reputation and the Basque Country's concentrated healthcare ecosystem, applicants competing for places in the same cycle are typically high-performing.

## Key Insight

Deusto Medicine is structurally the most academically meritocratic private Medicine programme among the options in this RAG system: admission is determined almost entirely by the quality of the academic transcript, with no proprietary test to prepare for. This makes it the natural first choice for students with consistently excellent academic records who want their secondary education performance to speak for itself — and the worst option for students hoping to compensate for an uneven academic history through test preparation.

The embedded mandatory Year 4 international placement, the PBL-from-Year-1 pedagogy, and the trilingual teaching environment represent genuine programme differentiators. The comparatively lower annual tuition (€17,850 vs sector average) combined with strong institutional prestige makes Deusto a cost-efficient option for high-performing candidates who qualify.

The primary strategic risk is timing: Deusto's admission window opens and closes much earlier than most programmes, and candidates who miss the regular period face structurally inferior options through the secondary window.

## Final Takeaway

Universidad de Deusto Medicine occupies a distinctive niche among Spanish private Medicine programmes: a Jesuit, PBL-based, person-centred programme in Bilbao with guaranteed international clinical exposure, trilingual teaching, and a transcript-primary admission model that rewards consistent academic excellence without a compensatory exam pathway.

Its strengths — institutional prestige, pedagogical differentiation, lower tuition, and embedded international placement — make it a highly attractive option for well-qualified candidates, particularly those with ties to the Basque Country or an interest in practising medicine there. Its risks — admission opacity, early application deadlines, limited MIR historical data, and non-disclosed hospital network — require careful due diligence before commitment.

**Is the programme in Spanish only?**
No. Teaching is delivered in Spanish, English (some subjects), and optionally Basque. Students choosing the Basque-language track can accumulate enough credits for official C1 Basque language profile recognition — relevant for Osakidetza professional access.

**What is PBL and how does Deusto use it?**
PBL (Problem-Based Learning / Aprendizaje Basado en Problemas) is a methodology where students learn theoretical content by working through real clinical cases in small groups facilitated by a tutor, rather than in large lectures. At Deusto, PBL is the primary teaching method from Year 1, requiring active participation and hypothesis-testing reasoning rather than passive note-taking.

**Is there clinical training from Year 1?**
Pre-clinical laboratory practice and simulation in the Virtual Hospital (DeustoBio facility) begin from Year 1. Real patient clinical rotations start from Year 3. A mandatory 2-month international clinical placement abroad is embedded in Year 4.

**When should I apply?**
Early. The regular application period for 2026–27 had already closed before May 2026, indicating the window likely opens in autumn of the prior year. Missing the regular period means relying on a special secondary period for remaining places, which is structurally more limited. Contact medicina@deusto.es well in advance of your target intake year.

**What hospitals will I train at?**
Deusto's clinical training is based in Bilbao, expected to involve hospitals within the Osakidetza network and private centres. The full confirmed list of affiliated clinical training partners should be requested directly from the Faculty of Health Sciences, as it is not currently confirmed from primary sources in this document.

**What career paths can I pursue after graduating?**
Graduates access the MIR examination directly, enabling specialisation in any medical specialty within Spain's public and private healthcare systems. Other paths include biomedical research (Deusto has 34 active research teams), hospital management, university teaching, military medicine, and international medical work. The Basque optional track adds value specifically for practice within Osakidetza.
