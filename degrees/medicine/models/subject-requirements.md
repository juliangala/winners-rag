# Subject background for Medicine admission in Spain

## Objective

This document defines how subject background affects:

- Eligibility (mainly in public universities)
- Performance and competitiveness (mainly in private universities)

---

## Core principle

Subject background has different roles depending on the admission pathway:

- Public universities → eligibility constraint
- Private universities → performance and evaluation factor

The system must not treat subject selection as a strict requirement for all universities.

---

## Key subjects

The main subjects relevant for Medicine are:

- Biology
- Chemistry
- Physics (in some cases)

---

## Public universities

### Role of subjects

Subjects are strongly linked to:

- PCE exams
- EvAU weighting
- Final admission grade

---

### Implications

- Biology + Chemistry → essential for competitiveness
- Missing key subjects → major limitation
- Direct impact on:
  - final grade
  - ranking

---

## Private universities

### Role of subjects

Most private universities:

- Do NOT require specific subjects formally
- Focus on:
  - academic record (Year 12)
  - admission tests
  - interviews

---

### However, subject background still matters

Subjects influence:

- Performance in admission exams
- Ability to pass scientific tests
- Academic readiness

---

### Implications

- Biology + Chemistry → strong advantage
- Missing scientific background → higher difficulty in admission process
- Does NOT necessarily block access, but increases risk

---

## Common scenarios

### 1. Biology + Chemistry

- Strongest profile
- Best alignment with:
  - public and private universities

---

### 2. Biology + Chemistry without Physics

- Fully viable in most cases
- Minor limitations

---

### 3. Weak or missing science background

- Public universities → very limited or not viable
- Private universities → still possible, but:

  - Higher difficulty in tests
  - Lower probability of success

---

## International systems

### British system (A-Levels)

- Strong specialization
- High depth in selected subjects

---

### French system

- Good general scientific alignment

---

### US system

- High variability
- Must be evaluated case by case

---

## Strategic implications

### Public universities

- Subject background directly affects:
  - eligibility
  - competitiveness

---

### Private universities

- Subject background affects:
  - probability of success
  - performance in admission tests
  - strategic fit

- But does NOT automatically exclude options

---

## Interaction with other models

### With profiles

- Academic level and subject background are independent
- A strong student may still lack scientific preparation

---

### With recommendation logic

- Public universities → subject check is mandatory (eligibility)
- Private universities → subject check adjusts feasibility, not eligibility

---

## Usage in recommendation logic

The system must:

- Apply subject constraints strictly for public universities
- Use subject background as a performance signal for private universities
- Avoid excluding private universities solely based on subject gaps

---

## Final principle

> Subject background defines eligibility in public universities, and probability of success in private universities.
